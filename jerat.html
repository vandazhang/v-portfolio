    Indonesia has a financial inclusion rate of 75% — meaning most adults have access to financial products. But financial literacy sits at just 65.4% (SNLIK 2024). That 9.6-point gap is exactly where BNPL and online lending thrives: products are used without being understood.

    The UK faces a structurally similar problem. BNPL is currently largely unregulated in the UK — Klarna, Clearpay, and Laybuy operate without the affordability checks that apply to credit cards or personal loans. FCA regulation has been proposed since 2021 but repeatedly delayed. The market is projected to triple by 2028.

    Both markets share a common mechanism: predatory design. False urgency timers, pre-selected credit defaults, and hidden fee structures bypass the deliberate decision-making that protects financial health. In both markets, younger users are most exposed.
